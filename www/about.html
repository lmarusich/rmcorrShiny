About this app

This Shiny app computes and visualizes repeated measures correlation (rmcorr). Rmcorr is the common within-individual linear association for paired, repeated measures data. Rmcorr is conceptually similar to a null multilevel model: A fixed slope and varying intercept by participant. For details about rmcorr and analyzing repeated measures data see below.

Alternative to rmcorr: Resources for multilevel modeling

Mulitlevel-modeling is a more complicated alternative to rmcorr, but multilevel modeling has capabilities that rmcorr does not. Multilevel modeling permits varying slopes whereas rmcorr always fits a common or fixed slope across participants. In addition, multilevel modeling can include additional variables (not just two dependent measures and participants) including more complex data structures (e.g., non-linear data, time-varying data, and more than two levels of nesting). See the Journal Article for rmcorr and references below more information.

  • Website (Freeman). Intuitive visual explanation of multilevel modeling
  • Journal Article (Aarts et al., 2014). Excellent overview on why repeated measures should be modeled and overview of multilevel modeling
  • Conference paper (Singmann, 2018). Informative tutorial on multilevel modeling
  • Book (Gelman and Hill, 2006). Comprehensive book on multilevel modeling with detailed explanations, equations, and examples using real data
